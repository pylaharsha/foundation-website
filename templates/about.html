Founder & Guiding Light

Dr. Chandra Bhanu Satpathy

We are guided by the vision of Dr. Chandra Bhanu Satpathy — a humanitarian, philosopher, and author whose life's work has been dedicated to the upliftment of communities through compassionate service, cultural enrichment, and ethical living.

His vision continues to inspire our foundation's mission across the Americas. He is the first Indian citizen invited by the US House of Representatives to open its session as Guest Chaplain (June 24, 2015).

Our Story

The Dr. Chandra Bhanu Satpathy Humanitarian Community Development Foundation of Americas was established to carry forward a singular vision: that communities flourish when supported materially, culturally, and educationally.


Inspired by the life and teachings of Dr. Chandra Bhanu Satpathy, we bring together volunteers, donors, and community leaders united in service. Our work spans a wide range of initiatives — from distributing meals at homeless shelters and running Book Drives for underserved students, to organizing classical dance performances, music workshops, and philosophy seminars for youth.


Through our Book Drive program, kids and youth collect, curate, and distribute books directly to learners in need — through schools, community centers, and shelter homes. Every book placed in a young reader's hands is a step toward a more equitable future.

Our Goals

  • Expand humanitarian relief and community development programs across the Americas
  • Build inclusive networks for cultural exchange, arts, and education
  • Support youth through scholarships, mentorship, and skill-building programs
  • Promote philosophical, ethical, and civic values in communities
  • Establishing inclusive community spaces and outreach initiatives that foster harmony, tolerance, pluralism, and service
  • Grow volunteer networks in underserved regions across the Americas
