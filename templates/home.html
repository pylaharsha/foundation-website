Our Founder & Guide

Dr. Chandra Bhanu Satpathy

A noted scholar, spiritual thinker, author, and humanitarian — Dr. C.B. Satpathy embodies the noble principles of love, empathy, compassion, and devotion. Under his inspiration, more than two hundred centers devoted to philanthropy, community development, and spiritualism are active across India, USA, Canada, Australia, South Africa, UK, and Europe.

He was invited by the US House of Representatives to open its session as a Guest Chaplain on June 24, 2015 — the first Indian citizen ever to receive such an honor.

About Us

Who we are, what we stand for, and where we are headed

Founder & Guiding Light

Dr. Chandra Bhanu Satpathy

We are guided by the vision of Dr. Chandra Bhanu Satpathy — a humanitarian, philosopher and spiritual leader whose life's work has been dedicated to the upliftment of communities through compassionate service, cultural enrichment, and spiritual growth.

His guiding light continues to inspire our foundation's mission across the Americas. He is the first Indian citizen invited by the US House of Representatives to open its session as Guest Chaplain (June 24, 2015).

Our Story

The Dr. CBS Humanitarian Community Development Foundation of Americas was established to carry forward a singular vision: that communities flourish when they are supported not only materially, but culturally, spiritually, and educationally. Inspired by the life and teachings of Dr. Chandra Bhanu Satpathy, we bring together volunteers, donors and community leaders united in service.


From distributing meals at homeless shelters to organizing classical dance performances and philosophy workshops for youth, our work is as diverse as the communities we serve.

Our Goals

  • Expand humanitarian relief programs across the Americas
  • Build a network of cultural exchange and arts education
  • Support youth through scholarships and mentorship
  • Promote philosophical and ethical values in communities
  • Establish centers for spiritual instruction and worship
  • Foster interreligious harmony and cultural understanding
  • Grow volunteer networks in underserved regions
